About Our Club

What Is Jiu Jitsu — And Why Does It Matter?

Jiu jitsu is a ground-based martial art built on one foundational idea: technique and leverage defeat size and strength. Unlike striking arts, jiu jitsu is designed to control and neutralize a threat — which makes it one of the most practical self-defense systems in the world. You don't need to be an athlete to start, and you don't need to be aggressive to be effective. What you'll develop over time is situational awareness, the ability to stay calm under physical pressure, and the confidence of knowing you can protect yourself and the people you love. These aren't small things.

If you're feeling a little nervous, that's exactly where you should be. Your first class is designed for people who have never stepped on a mat. No experience, no problem. We'll meet you where you are.

More Than Jiu Jitsu

This isn't just a martial arts class. It's a brotherhood. We are men from the same church, the same community, and the same faith — and we're choosing to do something hard together. There is a long tradition of men sharpening one another through shared physical challenge, and that's exactly what this is. You'll learn to trust the men on your left and right, to be vulnerable enough to struggle in front of each other, and to come out stronger for it.

The mats have a way of leveling everything. Titles, careers, and status don't mean much when you're on the ground learning something new. What's left is just men doing the work — and that's a powerful place to build real friendship.

The Spiritual Case for Training

Scripture calls us to be good stewards of what we've been given — and that includes our bodies. Physical discipline is one of the oldest forms of spiritual formation. When we train, we are practicing self-control, perseverance, humility, and courage — the same virtues we're called to cultivate in every area of life. There is something deeply clarifying about putting your body under stress in a controlled environment. It reveals who you are, and it gives you the opportunity to become more. Paul understood this: "I discipline my body and keep it under control" (1 Corinthians 9:27).

Our Philosophy

Iron Sharpens Iron (Proverbs 27:17). On these mats, we embrace the "Fog" — the pressure and chaos of a fight — so that we can find the clarity and peace of the "Dove" in our daily lives. In practice, that means you'll learn to breathe, think, and make decisions clearly when your body is under stress. That skill doesn't stay on the mat.

What to Expect in Your First Session

We'll start with foundational movement, cover a couple of basic techniques, and finish with some light positional drilling. No live sparring on day one. The goal is to get you comfortable on the mat, introduce you to how we train, and send you home wanting more.

Why Join LHBC JJ Club?

  • Community & Brotherhood: Train alongside men committed to growing together
  • Spiritual Formation: Develop virtues through physical discipline and challenge
  • No Experience Necessary: Classes designed for complete beginners
  • Mental Clarity: Learn to think clearly under pressure
  • Supportive Environment: Everyone progresses at their own pace with expert guidance
